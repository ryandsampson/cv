Ryan Sampson

Lead Estimator, East TN for Stansell Electric Company.

I am the Lead and only Estimator for Stansell Electric in East Tennessee. My work focuses on trasportation projects, such as traffic signals and airports, and heavy infrastructure, such as utility duct banks, overhead and underground electric utility work, and overhead and underground communication utility work. I also perform commercial and light industrial estimates for our fledgling CIM Department.

Recent Projects

  • City of Kingsport - Stone Drive and Bloomingdale Pike - Signal
    • Prime Project
    • $290K
    • Includes the removal of an existing span wire/strain pole instersection and installation of a new mast arm intersection. The project also includes various concrete items, pavement marking items, asphalt repair items, and boring items.
  • City of Kingsport - Main Street Roadway and Streetscape Improvements
    • Sub Project
    • $3.3M
    • Includes the removal of an existing light pole mounted signal and replacement with a new mast arm signal and new equipment. This project also includes the removal of 57 existing streetlight foundations and the installation of 83 new streetlight foundations along with the attendant conduit for feed of the streetlights. There is also the installation of a duct bank that includes conduits for Level 3, AEP Fiber Optic, AEP Power, City of Kingsport Fiber Optic, Charter Communications trunk lines, and CenturyLink trunk lines.
  • City of Sevierville - Parkway at Buc-ees
    • Sub Project
    • $700K
    • This project is the installation of a brand new mast arm signal at the intersection of Winfield Dunn Parkway and the access road for the new Buc-ees in the City of Sevierville. This project includes new mast arms, vehicle detection, CCTV video monitoring, and interconnection with the City's existing traffic observation system.
  • Town of Farragut - ATMS Phase I
    • Prime Project
    • $4.9M
    • This project is for the installation of a brand new ATMS or Advanced Traffic Management System for the Town of Farragut. This will include 10 miles of new fiber optic cabling, both aerial and underground, new controllers at all 26 intersections contained within the Town's system, new cabinets at 23 of the intersections, new radar detection for all intersections, removal of existing span wire/strain pole equipment and installation of new mast arm equipment at 5 intersections, installation of Road Side Units at all 26 intersections, new ethernet switches at all 26 intersections, system integration with the new Traffic Operations Center, and building of the new network for the interconnection system.
